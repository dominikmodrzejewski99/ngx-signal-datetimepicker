4. Reactive Forms (ControlValueAccessor)

The picker implements ControlValueAccessor, so it drops into classic @angular/forms exactly like a native input: [formControl], formControlName, or [(ngModel)] all work. disable() / enable() on the FormControl reach the picker via setDisabledState(); closing the panel marks the control as touched. Try the toggle below to see the disabled state flow through.

6. Wizard mode — date first, then time

Flip [wizard]="true" to turn the panel into a two-step flow: pick a date, then pick a time. After clicking a day the panel auto-advances to step 2; a Back button (or the step 1 chip in the header) returns to the calendar without discarding the value. Useful when the picker lives in a tight space or when you want a guided UX for less technical users.
